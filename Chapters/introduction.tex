\subsection{Number of satellites}

From the past analysis, we know that 8 satellites are required in an SSO orbit at 600 km with a sensor that has a spatial resolution of 50 m to re visit the AoI twice a day. Considering the lifetime of 3 years in the past section, and the mission lifetime of 8 years, this yields a minimum of 3 generation of satellites. This translates into \[3\times8=24\]
satellites. The next step looks further into details regarding reliability and deployment to minimize the number of satellites (this is the costlier element) and follow a launch strategy that take advantage of “bulk” launches to lower the deployment costs.

Costs for ground segment, based on scenario 11 with 8 operational satellites produces in the order of 100000 Mbit per day (see Figure 5 32) at 6 Mbps 

\[\frac{100000Mbit}{6Mbps}=16.67e3\left[s\right]=227[min]\]

Factor by 1/0.8 to get the communications time.

\[\frac{227\left[min\right]}{0.8}=347\left[min\right]\approx6[hrs]\]

So, we have about 6 hours of ground station time per day in order to download all the data generated by the eight satellites we need to have operational in order to fulfil the temporal resolution requirements. At 3 to 5 euros a min for S-band capable ground segment, we get an estimated.

\[6\left[\frac{hrs}{day}\right]\ast3\left[\frac{Euros}{min}\right]\ast365\left[\frac{day}{year}\right]\ast8\left[years\right]=3.156\ [MEuros]\]

This is 400 k Euros per year of operational mission. With these values, one could easily cover the costs of deploying a new ground station and converting the salaries.
